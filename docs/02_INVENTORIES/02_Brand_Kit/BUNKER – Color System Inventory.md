# BUNKER — Color System Inventory

Color is never decorative. Color encodes state, safety, and material truth.
This system is derived from civil-defense-era institutional palettes fused with industrial infrastructure realism.

---

## 1. The Signature Accent: Vault Yellow/Gold

**This is THE color that makes BUNKER read as BUNKER.**

Not optional. Not "one of several accents." The yellow/gold is the signature that separates a generic industrial space from a *facility*.

### 1.1 Primary Uses
- Safety railings and handrails
- Warning bands and caution stripes
- Door trim and hatch accents
- Facility numerals and zone identifiers
- Equipment handles and pull points
- Floor markings and directional guides

### 1.2 Tonal Range
- **VAULT GOLD** — Primary accent (warm, confident, reads at distance)
- **SAFETY YELLOW** — High-visibility warnings
- **AGED BRASS** — Worn/oxidized variant for older equipment

### 1.3 Doctrine
- Yellow never appears without structural purpose
- Yellow draws the eye to interaction points and safety-critical elements
- Yellow against steel grey is the foundational contrast pairing

---

## 2. Structural Palette (The Bones)

### 2.1 Steel Blue-Grey Range
The dominant structural color. Walls, floors, ceilings, equipment housings.

- **PLATE STEEL** — Primary structural surfaces (cool, muted)
- **BRUSHED METAL** — Equipment faces, console housings
- **DARK IRON** — Deeper shadows, recessed areas, conduits
- **OXIDIZED STEEL** — Controlled rust/wear at edges and joints

### 2.2 Concrete Range
Secondary structural surfaces. Floors, load-bearing walls, bunker shell.

- **CLEAN POUR** — Fresh/maintained concrete
- **STAINED GREY** — Age, moisture exposure, operational wear
- **PATCHED** — Repair areas, visible maintenance history

### 2.3 Application Doctrine
- Grey is the canvas; never the focus
- Structural color recedes; accent color advances
- Variation within grey range creates depth without distraction

---

## 3. Lighting-Derived Color (The Warmth)

**Critical insight: BUNKER is not cold and sterile.**

The facility has LIFE. Warmth comes from lighting, not decoration.

### 3.1 Tungsten Warm
- Desk lamps, work lights, residential areas
- Creates pools of warmth in operational spaces
- Humanizes the industrial shell

### 3.2 Fluorescent Cool
- Overhead panel lighting
- Corridor and circulation lighting
- Clinical/operational baseline

### 3.3 Mixed Lighting Doctrine
- Spaces should blend warm and cool sources
- Pure cold = medical/sterile (use sparingly)
- Pure warm = residential/comfort (private quarters)
- Mixed = operational reality (most spaces)

---

## 4. State Colors (Functional Truth)

State colors override all other accent considerations. When state is communicated, it takes precedence.

### 4.1 Lamp States
- **GREEN SOLID** — Stable / Ready / Nominal
- **AMBER PULSE** — Processing / Active / Working
- **DIM (no emission)** — Idle / Standby / Awaiting input
- **RED FLICKER** — Fault / Critical / Denied / Degraded

### 4.2 Band/Stripe States
- **GREEN BAND** — Cleared / Safe / Authorized
- **AMBER BAND** — Caution / Attention required
- **RED BAND** — Danger / Restricted / Do not operate
- **BLACK/YELLOW STRIPE** — Physical hazard / Pinch point / Moving equipment

### 4.3 Doctrine
- State color always tells truth
- State color overrides decorative consistency
- A red lamp means RED — no ambiguity, no "it's just the theme"

---

## 5. Warning & Hazard Bands

### 5.1 Caution (Yellow/Black)
- Moving equipment, pinch points, overhead clearance
- Diagonal stripe pattern

### 5.2 Warning (Orange/Black)
- Elevated risk, proceed with awareness
- Solid or striped

### 5.3 Danger (Red/White)
- Immediate hazard, authorization required
- High contrast for visibility

### 5.4 Restricted (Red/Black)
- Access controlled, clearance verification required
- Often paired with authorization signage

---

## 6. CRT/Terminal Emission Colors

The glow from screens and displays. Phosphor-derived, not LCD.

### 6.1 Primary Emission
- **PHOSPHOR GREEN** — Classic terminal output, nominal data
- **PHOSPHOR AMBER** — Alternate terminal variant, warmer tone

### 6.2 Accent Emission
- **CYAN/BLUE** — Secondary data, system messages
- **RED EMISSION** — Alerts, faults, critical notices

### 6.3 Doctrine
- CRT glow has bloom and persistence
- Emission colors are softer than painted colors
- Scanline presence affects perceived brightness

---

## 7. Material-Derived Color

Color that comes from the material itself, not applied paint.

### 7.1 Rust/Oxidation
- Controlled presence at wear points
- Tells story of age and moisture exposure
- Never decorative — always implies material history

### 7.2 Brass/Bronze
- Older equipment, legacy fixtures
- Warm metallic that complements vault yellow

### 7.3 Rubber/Gasket Black
- Door seals, cable grommets, anti-fatigue mats
- Functional black, not design black

---

## 8. Contrast & Readability Rules

### 8.1 Minimum Contrast
- Text on backgrounds must meet distance readability requirements
- Terminal text on CRT must account for bloom/glow reduction

### 8.2 Redundancy Doctrine
- **Color is never the only signal**
- Shape, text, and position must echo the message
- A colorblind operator must still understand state

### 8.3 Distance Hierarchy
- State lamps: readable across room
- Zone numerals: readable from corridor
- Console labels: readable at operational distance
- Detail text: readable at station

---

## 9. Prohibited Uses

- Decorative gradients
- Neon or saturated accent colors (except state red)
- Pure white as a background (too clinical, no material truth)
- Pure black as a background (no depth, no realism)
- Color for "branding" rather than function

---

## 10. Reference Pairings

**Primary Contrast Pair:**
Steel Blue-Grey + Vault Yellow/Gold

**State Communication:**
Grey background + Green/Amber/Red lamp

**Terminal Hierarchy:**
Dark housing + Phosphor emission + Scanline overlay

**Humanized Space:**
Cool structural + Warm tungsten pools + Plant green accents
