# BUNKER — Environment Inventory

This inventory catalogs the physical spaces and architectural elements that comprise the facility.
Every environment asset must support diegetic integrity and operational realism.

---

## 1. Signature Architectural Elements

### 1.1 Vault Door (THE Ceremony)
**The most important environmental asset in BUNKER.**

- Massive gear-door with bold facility numeral (centered, gold/yellow)
- Hydraulic arms on either side
- Yellow safety railings at platform edge
- Thick steel with visible segmentation and locking lugs
- Oxidation/wear on exterior surface (tells story of age)
- Interior face cleaner but still industrial

**This is the entry moment. Authority entering containment.**

### 1.2 Rounded Hatch Doorways
**The signature door geometry that makes BUNKER read as BUNKER.**

- Oval/rounded steel frames with thick rims
- Pressure-vessel geometry (not rectangular modern doors)
- Bulkhead aesthetic with visible locking mechanisms
- Warning placards flanking doorframe
- Status lamp above (room state indicator)
- Threshold plates with department markings

### 1.3 Airlock Chambers
- Double-door containment with sealing sequence
- Pressure gauges, air valve controls
- Decontamination elements (rinse hardware, timer)
- Warning bands on floor (yellow/black)
- Indicator lamps for seal status

## 2. Corridor Systems

### 2.1 Primary Spine Corridor
- Wide central circulation with high ceiling
- Exposed conduits and cable trays overhead
- Fluorescent panel lighting (cool)
- Floor markings: yellow directional lines, department zones
- Location plaques at intersections
- Emergency lighting strips (red, recessed)

### 2.2 Secondary Corridors
- Narrower, lower ceiling
- More exposed infrastructure
- Service access panels on walls
- Overhead pipe runs with department color coding

### 2.3 Service Passages
- Tight, utilitarian
- Bare concrete/steel
- Minimal lighting
- Cable runs and junction boxes prominent

## 3. Room Types

### 3.1 Command Theater (Primary Control Room)
- Raised console floor platform
- Central command console with mixed controls
- Observation window to adjacent bay (optional)
- Global state lamp prominently displayed
- Incident stack display
- Queue board
- Warm tungsten desk lighting mixing with overhead fluorescent

### 3.2 Equipment Bays
- Recessed alcoves with rack rails
- Status lamp rows on equipment faces
- Cable trays entering from above
- Service access plates
- Ventilation grilles (active cooling)
- Department signage

### 3.3 Archive/Storage Rooms
- Storage cages with controlled access
- Shelving units (steel, industrial)
- Ledger stations
- Retrieval carts
- Environmental controls (humidity/temp for sensitive storage)

### 3.4 Maintenance Shop
- Workbench with tool wall
- Parts shelving
- Calibration equipment
- Solder station
- Floor drains, anti-fatigue mats
- Bright task lighting

### 3.5 Security Checkpoint
- Badge reader station
- Keypad + key switch panel
- Authorization lamp
- Lockdown lever (guarded)
- Bulletproof glass partition (optional)
- Waiting area bench

## 4. Multi-Level Elements

### 4.1 Atrium Spaces
- Double or triple height
- Catwalks with yellow safety railings
- Views down into operational floors
- Exposed structural beams
- Large zone numerals on walls

### 4.2 Stairwells
- Industrial steel stairs with non-slip treads
- Yellow handrails
- Level markers at landings
- Emergency lighting
- Conduit runs along walls

### 4.3 Elevators/Lifts
- Industrial freight style
- Gate/accordion door or heavy sliding door
- Manual controls with confirmation
- Floor indicator (analog dial or lamp array)
- Weight limit placard

### 5.1 Ceiling Systems

## 6. Humanizing Elements

**Critical: Without these, the facility is a tomb.**

### 6.1 Plant Life
- Potted plants in common areas
- Ferns, hardy species (low light tolerant)
- Planter boxes built into architecture
- Function: morale, air quality, life presence

### 6.2 Furniture
- Chairs and benches in circulation areas
- Tables in break zones
- Worn but maintained
- Mix of periods (some older, some replaced)

### 6.3 Personal Artifacts
- Items on desks (photos, trinkets)
- Notice boards with posted items
- Bulletin boards with schedules, notices
- Evidence of daily human activity

### 6.4 Operational Life Signs
- Wet floor signs
- "LAUNDRY" and other mundane labels
- Coffee cups (not pristine)
- Clipboards with checklists
- Maintenance tags on equipment

---

## 7. Lighting Doctrine

### 7.1 Overhead (Cool)
- Fluorescent panels
- Industrial fixtures with guards
- Even, clinical baseline

### 7.2 Task Lighting (Warm)
- Desk lamps at stations
- Work lights in maintenance areas
- Pools of warmth in operational spaces

### 7.3 Emergency (Red)
- Recessed strips along baseboards
- Exit signs
- Activates during incidents

### 7.4 Status (Colored)
- Lamps on equipment and doors
- Green/amber/red state communication
- Always functional, never decorative

---

## 8. Material Palette

### 8.1 Primary Materials
- **Steel (blue-grey)**: walls, doors, equipment housings
- **Concrete**: floors, structural walls, bunker shell
- **Yellow/Gold**: railings, trim, numerals, markings

### 8.2 Secondary Materials
- Rubber gaskets on doors
- Glass in observation windows (thick, industrial)
- Plastic on older equipment (yellowed)
- Paper in documentation stations

### 8.3 Wear Patterns
- Scuffed paint at contact points
- Rust at moisture exposure areas
- Worn paths on floors
- Fingerprints on frequently touched surfaces

---

## 9. Signage Integration

Every environment includes:
- Room designation placard (at entry)
- Department seal (stamped or painted)
- Hazard warnings where applicable
- Emergency procedure notices
- Inspection tags on equipment

---

## 10. Environment States

Environments must support visual state changes:
- **Nominal**: standard lighting, green lamps
- **Alert**: amber accent lighting, caution overlays
- **Fault**: red emergency lighting, visible warnings
- **Lockdown**: restricted access indicators, sealed doors
