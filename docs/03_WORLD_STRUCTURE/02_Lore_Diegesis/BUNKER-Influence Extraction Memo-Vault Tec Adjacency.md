# BUNKER — Influence Extraction Memo (Vault‑Tec Adjacency)

Purpose: distill the *transferable doctrine* behind Vault‑style retro‑futurist bunker design into original, non-derivative rules that BUNKER can enforce at AAA fidelity.

---

## 1) Visual DNA (What the eye reads in 1 second)

### 1.1 Geometry
- Heavy, rounded-rect industrial framing: repeated ribs, arches, inset wall bays
- Thick bezels and “instrument housings” around screens
- Big numerals as identity (vault numbers / zone numbers)
- Symmetry and centered hero doors (doors are monuments)

### 1.2 Materials
- Painted steel panels with scuffs and seam lines
- Concrete massing on exteriors; utilitarian cladding
- Rubber gaskets, conduit bundles, cable trays
- Glass that feels thick and imperfect (instrument glass, not phone glass)

### 1.3 Color Blocking
- Neutral industrial base (steel/concrete)
- High-contrast safety trims (hazard yellow bands)
- Saturated institutional accent blocks (clean color fields, applied like paint, not lighting)

### 1.4 Lighting
- Overhead practicals (fluorescent/industrial fixtures)
- Clean interior light that reveals construction details
- Exterior sun-bleach / dust haze contrast when outside is present

---

## 2) Institutional Tone DNA (What the world “sounds like”)

### 2.1 Emotional Contradiction (Core Engine)
- **Optimism as doctrine** paired with **menace as subtext**
- Smiling certainty. Procedural calm. Unquestioned authority.

### 2.2 Copy Cadence Rules
- Short sentences.
- Declarative language.
- Instructional certainty.
- Safety framed as destiny.

### 2.3 Propaganda Mechanics (Transferable)
- Safety messaging everywhere (placards, manuals, posters)
- Ritualized compliance (forms, stamps, inspections)
- “Model citizen” framing (operator as responsible steward)

---

## 3) UI Grammar (Diegetic Interface Rules)

### 3.1 The UI is a Device
- The interface is always *contained* by a physical frame (bezel, screws, vents, knobs)
- Screens are treated as “readouts,” not canvases

### 3.2 Navigation as Tabs + Modes
- Mode switches feel like hardware:
  - tabs / plates / hard buttons
  - visible active state lamp
  - minimal animation; mechanical settle

### 3.3 Typography & Layout
- Monospace telemetry for data
- Condensed industrial headings for labels
- Strict column alignment; numbers as first-class citizens

### 3.4 Feedback as Physical Events
- clicks, relays, lamp pulses
- stamps/receipts in logs
- no playful confirmations

---

## 4) Facility Spatial Storytelling

### 4.1 Doors are Narrative
- Entry doors communicate:
  - access control
  - permanence
  - consequence

### 4.2 Comfort Inside Infrastructure
- “Human life” elements exist *inside* industrial structure:
  - gardens/atriums
  - seating corners
  - personal artifacts
- The contrast is the point.

---

## 5) What BUNKER Adopts (Doctrine)

### 5.1 What We Mirror
- Monumental entry door + zone numeral system
- Painted safety trims + industrial neutrals
- Repeating corridor ribs + inset bays
- Device-framed UI with tabs/modes
- Propaganda-as-procedure (manuals, stamps, inspections)

### 5.2 What BUNKER Must Make Original
- No Vault‑Tec marks, slogans, mascots, or named entities
- No 1:1 palette or layout replication
- No recognizable “Pip‑Boy” casing silhouette

---

## 6) BUNKER Signature Differentiators (So it becomes its own IP)

### 6.1 Brand Entity (In‑World Builder)
- Replace corporate mascot energy with **civil-infrastructure authority**.
- Proposed builder archetype: **Continuity Directorate / Civil Systems Bureau** (name pending).

### 6.2 Color Doctrine
- Keep hazard yellow as *safety trim*.
- Shift primary accent away from Vault-blue into **facility-grade industrial**:
  - deep oxide teal / graphite blue-gray / muted military green
  - CRT emissions in amber/green reserved for readouts

### 6.3 UI Signature
- Tabs are **plates**.
- Global state is a **lamp + header band**.
- Primary navigation is the **Facility Schematic**.

### 6.4 Copy Signature
- Less “sales brochure.” More “operations manual.”
- Procedural, minimal, high-authority.

---
